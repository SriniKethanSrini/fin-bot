Central Government Health Schemes

National Health Mission (NHM)

Description:

The National Health Mission (NHM) is a flagship program of the Indian government launched in 2013. It aims to address the health needs of underserved rural areas and vulnerable populations by providing accessible, affordable, and quality healthcare services. NHM encompasses various health initiatives, including maternal and child health, immunization, communicable diseases control, non-communicable diseases prevention and control, and health system strengthening.

How to Apply for the Scheme:

To apply for NHM programs or services, individuals can typically reach out to their nearest government health facility, such as primary health centers (PHCs), community health centers (CHCs), or district hospitals. These facilities serve as the primary points of contact for accessing NHM services. Citizens can inquire about specific health programs, avail health services, register for health camps link for apply. You will need to provide proof of identity and BPL status to enroll in the scheme.
